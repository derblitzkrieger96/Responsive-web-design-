Albert Einstein

The man who revolutioned physics forever

  • 1915 Publishes the general theory of relativity, providing a new understanding of gravity and proposing the concept of spacetime curvature.
  • 1919 Experimental confirmation of general relativity during a solar eclipse, validating Einstein's predictions about light bending around massive objects.
  • 1921 Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, which contributed to the development of quantum mechanics.
  • 1922 Proposes the concept of stimulated emission, which forms the basis of the development of lasers.
  • 1933 Leaves Germany due to the rise of the Nazi regime and settles in the United States as a professor at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton.
  • 1939 Signs a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt, urging research into atomic energy and warning about the potential development of atomic weapons.
  • 1940 Becomes a U.S. citizen, maintaining his position at the Institute for Advanced Study.
  • 1945 Witnesses the detonation of the first atomic bomb during the Manhattan Project, expressing concern about the destructive power of nuclear weapons.
  • 1952 Offered the presidency of the newly formed state of Israel, which he declines.
  • 1955 Passes away on April 18 at the age of 76 in Princeton, leaving behind a remarkable legacy in physics and scientific inquiry.
  • 1979 Einstein's brain is studied by scientists after being preserved following his death, leading to insights about the structure and organization of his brain.
  • 1999 Time magazine names Albert Einstein the "Person of the Century" for his contributions to science and his influence on our understanding of the universe.
  • 2015 The LIGO (Laser Interferometer Gravitational-Wave Observatory) detects gravitational waves, providing direct evidence for Einstein's general theory of relativity.
  • 2016 Einstein's prediction of gravitational waves is confirmed with the discovery of gravitational waves originating from the merger of two black holes.
  • 2019 Celebrations worldwide mark the 100th anniversary of the confirmation of general relativity and the eclipse measurements that made Einstein a scientific celebrity.
